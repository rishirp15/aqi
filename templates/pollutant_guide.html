Guide to Common Air Pollutants

Understand the pollutants that affect the Air Quality Index.

PM2.5

Fine Particulate Matter

Tiny inhalable particles smaller than 2.5 micrometers. They can penetrate deep into the lungs and enter the bloodstream, posing the greatest health risk.

Common Sources

Vehicle exhaust, power plants, wildfires, industrial processes, and cooking.

Primary Health Effects

Lung irritation, asthma attacks, reduced lung function, and increased risk of heart attacks and strokes.

O₃ (Ozone)

Ground-Level Ozone

A harmful pollutant created by chemical reactions between Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) and Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) in the presence of sunlight.

Common Sources

Not emitted directly. Forms from vehicle exhaust, industrial emissions, and chemical solvents on hot, sunny days.

Primary Health Effects

Chest pain, coughing, throat irritation, and worsening of conditions like asthma and bronchitis.

CO (Carbon Monoxide)

Colorless, Odorless Gas

A toxic gas formed by the incomplete combustion of carbon-based fuels. It reduces the amount of oxygen that can be transported in the bloodstream.

Common Sources

Vehicle exhaust, gas stoves, generators, and burning wood, coal, or propane.

Primary Health Effects

Headaches, dizziness, and nausea at low levels. Can be fatal at high concentrations, especially indoors.

NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide)

Reactive Gas

A highly reactive gas that contributes to smog and acid rain. It can inflame the lining of the lungs and reduce immunity to lung infections.

Common Sources

Emissions from cars, trucks, buses, power plants, and off-road equipment.

Primary Health Effects

Aggravates respiratory diseases like asthma, leading to wheezing, coughing, and difficulty breathing.

SO₂ (Sulfur Dioxide)

Pungent Gas

A gas produced from burning fossil fuels like coal and oil. It can harm the respiratory system and make breathing difficult, especially for children and asthmatics.

Common Sources

Fossil fuel combustion at power plants, industrial facilities, and from locomotive and ship engines.

Primary Health Effects

Affects the respiratory system, particularly lung function. Can irritate the eyes and worsen asthma.

PM10

Coarse Particulate Matter

Inhalable particles with diameters of 10 micrometers or smaller. They are generally less harmful than PM2.5 as they don't penetrate as deeply.

Common Sources

Dust from construction sites, landfills, agriculture, wildfires, and pollen.

Primary Health Effects

Can irritate the eyes, nose, and throat. Can worsen conditions like asthma and heart disease.
