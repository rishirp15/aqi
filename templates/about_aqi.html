The Air Quality Index (AQI) Explained

What is AQI?

The AQI is a standardized, color-coded scale that simplifies air pollution data. It takes measurements of several major pollutants and converts them into a single, comprehensive number, communicating the associated health risk over a short period.

How it's Calculated

The final AQI value is determined by the highest sub-index recorded among the key pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, O₃, NO₂, SO₂, CO, NH₃). The pollutant with the worst reading is called the "major pollutant."

{# --- AQI CATEGORIES (Color-Coded and Improved) --- #}

AQI Categories & Health Risk Scale

Good (0 - 50)

Air quality is satisfactory. **Little or no risk.**

Action: Enjoy outdoor activities.

Satisfactory (51 - 100)

Air quality is acceptable. Minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people.

Action: Unusually sensitive people should limit prolonged outdoor exertion.

Moderate (101 - 200)

Discomfort to sensitive groups (children, elderly, heart/lung disease).

Action: Sensitive groups should significantly reduce heavy exertion.

Poor (201 - 300)

Breathing discomfort to most people on prolonged exposure. **Health warning.**

Action: Reduce outdoor exposure; wear N95 masks outside.

Very Poor (301 - 400)

Respiratory illness risk increased for everyone. **High Alert.**

Action: Avoid all outdoor physical activity.

Severe (401+)

Affects healthy people and seriously impacts those with existing diseases. **Emergency conditions.**

Action: Remain indoors. Use air purifiers.
